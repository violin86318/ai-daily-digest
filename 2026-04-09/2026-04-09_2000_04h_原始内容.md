## 来源1: AI数据中心与电价研究

**来源**: QuantoSei News
**URL**: https://news.quantosei.com/2026/04/09/new-study-finds-no-link-between-rising-electricity-rates-and-ai-data-centers/

Are booming AI data centers causing your electricity costs to soar? A comprehensive new analysis debunks this widespread concern, revealing no measurable link between the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence infrastructure and rising consumer electricity rates across the United States.

Key findings:
- No statistically significant correlation between number of data centers in a state and electricity prices
- States with highest data center concentration had virtually identical electricity prices to national average
- States with faster economic growth saw lower electricity rate increases (20% vs 39.4% from 2015-2025)

US data centers consumed 76 TWh in 2018, jumping to ~176 TWh in 2023 (131% increase). Meta's consumption rose from 6.97 TWh (2020) to 18.06 TWh (2024) - a 159% jump.

Top 10 data center states averaged 14.46¢/kWh in 2025, nearly identical to 14.39¢/kWh for all other states.

## 来源2: ESRI爱尔兰AI研究

**来源**: RTE
**URL**: https://www.rte.ie/news/analysis-and-comment/2026/0409/1567304-ai-analysis-esri/

A new study from ESRI and Department of Finance found "winners and losers" in AI adoption:

Bad news:
- ~7% of current jobs could be displaced in short to medium term
- Job losses concentrated among highly educated workers (IT, customer service, clerical support)
- AI adoption likely to widen income inequality

Good news:
- Remaining workers likely to see wage increases from productivity gains
- Customer-facing or physically demanding roles face low substitution risk (builders, refuse workers, health professionals)
- Ireland's tax/welfare system well-placed to absorb short-term income losses
